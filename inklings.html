The Herald's Bench blazon a shield · DEV

Blazon is the centuries-old language for describing coats of arms — a real, tightly-constrained grammar. Here you write a valid blazon from words on the right; it's drawn as a shield (to emblazon), and equipping it grants passive bonuses read from what's on it.

A blazon is read in a fixed order, describing the shield from the background up:

  1. Field — the background colour. It can be split by a division: party per pale (a vertical split) or per fess (horizontal), giving two tinctures.
  2. Ordinary (optional) — a bold geometric band: chief (top strip), fess (middle bar), pale (vertical bar), bend (diagonal), chevron (∧), or bordure (border).
  3. Charge group (optional) — the emblems on top: a number (a / two / three), a charge (lion, eagle, tower, cross…), an optional attitude (its pose, e.g. rampant), and its own tincture.

Tinctures come in three kinds: metals (Or = gold, Argent = silver), colours (Gules, Azure, Sable, Vert, Purpure), and furs (Ermine, Vair).

⚖ The rule of tincture — heraldry's one hard agreement rule: never place a metal on a metal or a colour on a colour (poor contrast). Furs are neutral and break no rule. The exception is counterchanged: on a divided field an element can flip the two field tinctures across the line — legal even where it'd otherwise clash. The Herald flags any violation, and you can't equip an illegal blazon.

e.g. “Party per pale Azure and Or, a chevron counterchanged, three mullets Or.”

How your shield's powers are read

Every part of the blazon is also a power. Equip a shield and these become passive buffs.

Metal = polarity (which way the shield leans):
Or (Strike / sun) → offense (+attack)  ·  Argent (Ward / moon) → defense (+guard).

Colour = element (a themed buff). The elements sit in a counter-cycle (fire → vine → stone → storm → water → fire; each beats the next — lore for now, kept for a future duel):
Gules · fire → attack   Vert · vine → reach   Sable · stone → guard   Purpure · storm → haste   Azure · water → hearts.

Attitude = tempo (an intensity + speed slider): rampant/salient (fast) lean aggressive — more attack & swing speed; couchant/guardant (slow) lean defensive — more guard. Higher tempo always = more haste.

Word order: colour on the charge = figure (burst, +attack); colour as the field = ground (sustained, +guard).

Charge = role (its main stat, scaled by the number a/two/three):
🦁 Lion → damage (attack)   🦅 Eagle → vision & range (reach)   🏰 Tower → bulwark (guard)   ✝ Cross → vitality (hearts)
★ Mullet → far reach   ☾ Crescent → nightward (guard)   🌹 Rose → vitality (hearts)   🕊 Martlet → swift (haste).

The Bench shows the live element · polarity · tempo of whatever you're building, above the passive stats.

The Curator

The library is the Nouns wing — every word shelved here is a noun (verbs, adjectives and adverbs get their own homes elsewhere). It's a tree of meanings: every noun is filed by what it is a kind of, so the shelves themselves teach a real relationship linguists call hyponymy. Each book is a broader idea; the words shelved on it are its narrower kinds.

A book like Bird is a hypernym (the general term). robin, wren, finch are its hyponyms (the specific kinds). Words that share a shelf are co-hyponyms — siblings under the same parent.

Open any collected word (tap it on a shelf page) to see how it connects to other nouns:

  • ↑Hypernym — a broader kind. A robin is a kind of bird → animal.
  • ↓Hyponym — a narrower kind. Kinds of bird: robin, wren, owl…
  • ⊂Meronym — a part of it. A bird has a beak, a wing.
  • ⊃Holonym — the whole it belongs to. A wing is part of a bird.
  • ⇄Antonym — the opposite. A few nouns pair up (absence ⇄ presence, ability ⇄ inability).

🌳 Why it matters: these links turn your loose pile of nouns into a map. Follow a word up to its hypernyms and you generalise; follow it down to hyponyms and you get specific. Explore parts and wholes and you start to see how the whole dictionary hangs together.

Opposites are really the adjective's & adverb's game (hot ⇄ cold) — over at the Apothecary, an adjective's antonym is one end of its dumbbell, and its synonyms grow the flask. Nouns just borrow the odd antonym.

Collected relatives are clickable — hop word to word to wander the tree. Words you haven't found yet show greyed out, as a hint of what's still out there.

Ink & potions. Spelling words pays off by part of speech:

  • Spell a noun → earn ink (your currency, shown top-left). Spend it at the Stall on the home screen (walk up and press E, or tap SHOP) to grow your satchel.
  • Spell an adjective → brew a potion that matches its meaning. Each adjective sits on a “dumbbell” — an attribute (speed, size, light) with two opposite ends: a self-buff you drink and an antonym that (soon) debuffs every beast on screen. So swift→Speed, big→Size, bright→Reveal; their opposites slow/small/dark brew the debuff potions. Potions stack in the HUD.

Flasks (the Apothecary — P / ⚗️). Each potion has a flask whose size = how many uses it holds. Collect more synonyms of a pole to grow its flask; spell the attribute noun (speed/size/light) for a bonus. A full flask can’t brew more until you drink one.

Drink a self-buff potion with 1/2/3 or by tapping its HUD button — each lasts 25 seconds:

  • Size (from big) — grow bigger, with longer attack reach.
  • Speed (from swift) — move faster.
  • Reveal (from bright) — the minimap shows the whole map and which screens still have letters.

Rewards repeat: re-spelling a word spends its letters again and pays again.

Word of the Day. Each day the Wordsmithy shows a bonus word — always built from letters you've unlocked, so you can always collect what you need. Spell it once for a big ink bonus.

Mad Libris. The lectern right of the Wordsmithy. You start with one fable; recover the rest one at a time — beasts rarely drop a page, or bind the next one at the Stall with materials beasts drop. Restore a story by filling each blank with a collected word that matches the needed part of speech (noun/verb/adjective/adverb). Matching the original word earns bonus ink. A word can be used in a chapter only once, ever — so keep learning new ones. Finish every recovered chapter to add the book to your Wordhoard.

Creatures & the bestiary (B). Out in the field you'll meet more than inklings — pests, cubes, and hazards that drop book-binding materials (paper, glue, thread, wax, leather…). Beat one and its loot drops on the ground — walk over it to collect (the Magnet feat later makes drops drift to you) — but grab it before The Kindle scorches it. Cheese heals a heart. The bestiary logs every creature you've beaten and reveals more (stats, drops, lore) the more of that creature you defeat.

Feats (C). Collecting verbs levels you up along five word-count milestones per category (1, then every 5). Rewards interleave passive boosts and abilities: Motion → move speed + Slide; Competition → attack + Finisher/Combo; Perception → Divine (a daily letter peek in the library) + Treasure Sense (drops glint); Possession → satchel space + Magnet. More verb categories arrive later.
